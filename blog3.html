Balancing Teaching, Performing, and Research

Many musicians today wear multiple hats. We teach, we perform, we compose, we record, and sometimes we research or write as well. On paper this sounds inspiring; in reality it can be tiring and confusing to manage.

For me, the balance started to make sense when I stopped thinking of these roles as separate boxes and started seeing how they feed one another. A concept I discover while researching shrutis might become an explanation in class. A doubt raised by a student might turn into a research question. A performance challenge might reveal a gap in my practice routine or teaching method.

Practically, balance comes down to clear boundaries and realistic expectations. It’s not possible for every day to contain equal parts of all three roles. Some weeks lean more towards teaching, others towards performances or writing. Instead of chasing a perfect daily mix, I try to look at balance across a longer time frame — weeks or months.

Another useful idea is to keep a simple “parking lot” notebook. Whenever a thought for a class, a future concert, or a research idea comes up at the wrong time, I write it down quickly instead of switching tasks. This way I respect the current role I’m in, while still capturing ideas for later.

Balancing multiple roles will always be a moving target. But when each role is allowed to inform and strengthen the others, the workload starts to feel less like a battle and more like a single, connected journey.
